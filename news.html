Intelligence Watch

U.S. opens temporary Iran oil-sanctions window as talks continue

Reuters reports that the United States issued a temporary 60-day general license allowing Iranian crude oil and petroleum-related transactions through 21 August 2026 as part of negotiations with Tehran. Follow-up reporting says Iran disputes U.S. claims about who controls unfrozen assets and whether the money must be spent on U.S. goods.

Why it matters: The story sits inside the sanctions-intelligence lane: oil flows, banking permissions, Strait of Hormuz pressure, U.S.-Iran diplomacy, and the border between national security policy and economic leverage.
