# Public Policy Convergence Tracker

Updated: 2026-06-29

## Purpose
A public-record tracker for policies, mandates, standards, pilots, contracts, treaties, and institutions that centralise identity, money, movement, speech, security, health, energy, land, food, and governance.

## Boundary
This tracker follows public records and institutional actions. It does not claim secret coordination. Each item must be linked to an actor, date, jurisdiction, source class, evidence level, and plain-language boundary.

## Source Hierarchy
- Law, regulation, treaty, court order, or official policy document
- Government procurement, public contract, budget, hearing, or committee record
- Central bank, BIS, IMF, World Bank, OECD, G20, or official monetary document
- Corporate filing, annual report, procurement notice, or investor disclosure
- NGO, foundation, standards-body, or public-private partnership document
- Major dated reporting that links to source documents
- Open-source lead requiring verification

## Signal Levels
- Level 1: Mention — The topic appears in a source and is tracked as a lead.
- Level 2: Pilot — A sandbox, trial, grant, or memorandum exists.
- Level 3: Procurement — Money, vendors, standards, or implementation plans are visible.
- Level 4: Mandate — A rule, law, treaty, or institutional requirement exists.
- Level 5: Integration — Two or more systems connect, such as identity, money, health, travel, speech, policing, or AI.
- Level 6: Lock-in — Opt-out becomes difficult because services, travel, work, money, or compliance depend on the system.

## Lanes
- Agenda 2030 / Global Standards / Policy Harmonisation: Track how broad international goals, standards, treaties, model laws, funding conditions, and public-private partnerships become domestic policy. Question: Are national policies being aligned through global standards without clear democratic oversight?
- Digital ID / Biometrics / Identity Wallets: Track when identity becomes the gate to banking, travel, health records, government services, age verification, work, benefits, or online access. Question: Is identity becoming a master permission key?
- CBDC / Tokenized Money / Programmable Finance: Track when money becomes programmable, identity-linked, surveilled, restricted, or routed through central-bank and compliance rails. Question: Is money becoming a permission layer?
- War / Security / Emergency Powers: Track when war, cyber threats, terror threats, pandemics, climate emergencies, migration crises, or energy shocks expand permanent security powers. Question: Is crisis converting civilian life into permanent security architecture?
- Speech / Platforms / Search / Payment Access: Track rules and partnerships that shape what can be posted, searched, ranked, monetised, advertised, or paid for online. Question: Is speech control being routed through platform and payment infrastructure?
- Health Governance / Biosecurity / Emergency Health Powers: Track when health systems connect to identity, travel, work, emergency law, procurement, data systems, and speech policy. Question: Is public health becoming a governance operating system?
- Climate / Energy / Carbon / Mobility: Track when climate and energy policy move into travel limits, energy rationing, consumption scoring, land controls, ESG finance, carbon markets, food policy, and mobility rules. Question: Are energy and climate systems becoming a behaviour-management layer?
- Migration / Borders / Biometric Travel: Track how border systems, biometrics, digital identity, international law, NGOs, labour markets, housing pressure, and security narratives reshape movement and citizenship. Question: Is movement being managed through identity, biometric, and security infrastructure?
- Food / Water / Land / Supply Chains: Track when farms, land, water, seed, fertilizer, meat, logistics, food standards, and supply chains concentrate into corporate-state policy networks. Question: Are food and land systems becoming control infrastructure instead of local resilience?

## Reader Method
- Find the lane before drawing conclusions.
- Attach actor, date, jurisdiction, source class, signal level, and boundary.
- A speech is level 1, a pilot is level 2, a contract is level 3, a mandate is level 4, integration is level 5, lock-in is level 6.
- When three or more lanes converge around one actor, law, platform, crisis, or institution, flag it as a convergence event.
- Track institutions, offices, corporations, laws, money, contracts, and named public actors; do not blame populations.
